I still remember the night of the December 31, 2019, a chilly winter night at my native home. It was the the time when I was looking at the clock on my mobile screen and thinking, "just few more minutes before the whole world starts welcoming the new magical number 2020 with new hope and new plans.". I too had a lot of plans to execute with a lot of hope and expectations for favorably eventful days ahead. I am not going to put out a survey of events (positives or otherwise) that followed.

Yes, we started our new year 2020 with two most disastrous events in the world: (a) Australian bushfire that destroyed over 20 million hectors of plants lives, over five thousands houses and killed tens of thousands of precious lives (humans, animals and birds); (b) the start of an outbreak of the novel coronavirus (COVID-19) in the city of Wuhan, China. It felt as though the mother Earth is telling: "Oh, Human! Please stop your torturous actions on me, or else there will be consequences like these."
